# Agent Continuity Kit

## Your AI agent works today. Will it recover tomorrow?

A practical toolkit for auditing memory, permissions, recovery, and continuity in AI agents, RAG systems, and personal automation workflows.

Most AI agents fail quietly.

They remember facts but lose obligations. They summarize the project but forget the constraints that made the project trustworthy. They look coherent after reset, but what comes back is not necessarily bound by what mattered before.

The Agent Continuity Kit helps you find those failure points.

## The core problem

A long chat history is not continuity.
A vector database is not continuity.
A polished persona is not continuity.

Continuity exists when the system remains constrained by prior commitments, corrections, permission boundaries, and recovery rules after reset, migration, context loss, or tool failure.

## What you get

The full kit includes:

- **25-question continuity checklist** — score your agent before trusting it.
- **Recovery bundle template** — define what must survive restart/migration.
- **Risk matrix template** — map failures by severity, likelihood, evidence, and fix.
- **Memory hygiene template** — decide what to store, promote, retire, or reject.
- **Permission ledger template** — separate capability from authority.
- **Reset/migration drill** — test whether behavior survives pressure.
- **Mini case study** — a compact example from a live OpenClaw continuity agent.

After using the kit, you should know:

- where your agent loses continuity;
- which memories actually matter;
- which actions need permission gates;
- whether your checks measure reality or theatre;
- what minimum recovery bundle your system needs;
- what to fix before increasing autonomy.
